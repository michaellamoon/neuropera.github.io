Why We March

Abigail Whitman

Project Collaborators

“Hombres Necios” performed by Rosa Evangelina and Gabriella Padilla. Improvisation performed by Tracy Thompson. Visual Editing by Olivia Popejoy, Katelyn Maheia. Imagery provided by Gabriella Padilla, Tyler Sanders, Simone Zapata, Menghe Jing, Rosa Evangelina, Tracy Thompson, Jordyn Foley, Abigail Whitman, Gina Whitman, and The Atlantic.

Project Description

Abigail Whitman’s “Why We March” is an excerpt from a larger work exploring social justice through the women’s rights movement. This excerpt explores why we fight for change, both on a historical and personal level. It explores the method of peaceful protest in the push for social justice.

Lyrics


English:
You foolish men who lay
the guilt on women,
not seeing you're the cause
of the very thing you blame;

You fight their stubbornness,
With gravity,
you say it was their lightness
when it was your guile.

Patent is your arrogance
that fights with many weapons
since in promise and insistence
you join world, flesh and devil


Spanish:
Hombres necios que acusáis
a la mujer, sin razón,
sin ver que sois la ocasión
de lo mismo que culpáis;

Combatís su resistencia
y luego, con gravedad,
decís que fue liviandad
lo que hizo la diligencia.

Bien con muchas armas fundo
que lidia vuestra arrogancia,
pues en promesa e instancia
juntáis diablo, carne y mundo.
